Long

Rad Winds is an experimental wind-energy game and digital-twin sandbox built in Bevy/Rust. Explore turbine test ranges, inspect 3D asset rails, select individual or grouped turbines, view SCADA-style turbine data, and test evolving MBDyn structural turbine rigs. This is an active technical playtest build. Expect experimental systems, placeholder visuals, and ongoing performance tuning.

Current playtest focus: Phase 3 is now stabilized around turbine selection, grouped SCADA, camera controls, asset rails, vortex rooms, and structural rooms.

Phase 4 is starting now and focuses on scalable MBDyn turbine tiers:

  • Visual proxy turbines
  • Lightweight MBDyn rigs
  • Active runtime rigs
  • Full focus/scientific rigs for detailed inspection

Current Playtest Features

  • First-person and fly-camera exploration
  • Selectable wind turbines and MBDyn turbine rigs
  • Drag-select multiple turbines
  • Shift + number turbine group assignment
  • Double-tap number keys to recall turbine groups
  • Combined group SCADA panel with summed power and averaged rotor/health data
  • Lightweight and full-detail MBDyn turbine visualization tiers
  • Asset rail map for reviewing imported 3D models
  • Animated asset previews where supported
  • Vortex particle test rooms
  • Structural blade/airfoil visualization rooms
  • Music radio controls and in-game UI control list
  • Weather, clouds, terrain, missions, and turbine collapse/destruction test systems

About the developer

Danny Sale is a wind-energy engineer with 18+ years spanning blade design, aerodynamics, testing, including years spent as wind turbine technicians & researcher. He led aerodynamics and control systems projects from 2 MW to 20 MW+ wind turbine platforms supporting their certification & campaigns. Combining certified field experience with advanced engineering expertise, he has now turned to game design to let his creativity go wild.
